Week 1

Day 1 | 18 July 2023


Scheduled our first meeting with our client on Wednesday, 19 July 2023 at 10 am. We have crafted and refined our questions for the requirements-gathering session with our client tomorrow.

Today, our team learned about mental models and how they apply to computing science and gathering the requirements of stakeholders in our project. Moreover, we have learned to ask good questions using the Mum Test which helped our team craft questions that do not provide generic responses such as yes and no.

We are also in the midst of crafting our team contract.

The questions prepared by the team for Barclays are:

  1. What is your reason for creating an autodonate app - what is the goal you want to achieve?
  2. What would you define as success for this project?
  3. What makes you different from other donation applications? (Goal of project)
  4. Based on your customer demographic, how likely do you think users are to adopt this application and what can make this number higher?
  5. What methods could be implemented to accurately track users' spending habits and determine appropriate donation amounts?
  6. How can we incentivize users to use the app regularly and maintain their donation commitments?
  7. Are there any legal or regulatory considerations that need to be considered when handling financial transactions and charitable donations?
  8. Are there any specific branding or design guidelines that need to be followed to align with Barclays' image and values?
  9. How can the app leverage spending habits to determine suitable charities for individuals? Are there any existing data sources or APIs that can be utilised?
  10. Are there any existing charity partners you would like us to focus on?
  11. Limitations of charity donations

Technical Requirements

  1. What features would users expect in the Auto Donate app? Such as goal tracking, impact stories.
  2. Is there any preference for the Auto Donate App being a mobile application or web application? If so, what?
  3. Would the app be integrated within the Barclays banking application, or would it be a separate application?
  4. Are there any specific accessibility or inclusivity requirements that the app should meet?
  5. Which payment processer should we use? And would there be any test APIs for us to use?
  6. How might the app integrate with existing payment systems and platforms to streamline the donation process

Day 3 | 20 July 2023


Following the client's meeting yesterday, the team came out with the meeting minutes for the Requirement Gathering meeting. Additionally, the team worked on the prototype based on the client's requirement raised during the meeting. The team had came out with 2 paper prototypes. A photo of the 2 prototype can be seen here:

The team had incorporated the Barclays' mobile application look and feel into the prototype to provide a seamless integration with Barclays' existing application. Prototype 1 features asking user about its interest in the causes that they are passionate about and a curated feed will be shown to the user about relevant causes they have indicated. In each cause, there is goal tracking to show the total amount of money required for the cause and how much has been raised so far. The first prototype allows the user to choose either an upfront amount or a recurring payment. Prototype 2 features a more generic UI, allowing the user to filter the causes and find causes that they might be passionate about. A donation history page is also featured to allow users to track their donation history, with a button for the user to make the donation again.

The prototype will be sent to the client and will be further refined with comments and feedbacks from the client.

Day 4 | 21 July 2023


Today the team had a lecture on prototyping. We had learnt about a plethora of prototyping techniques such as the rapid prototyping and paper-based, some of which are recap of the Human Computer Interaction module. The team plans to incorporate rapid prototyping in conjunction with interactive mockups later, through the use of Figma, into our prototyping process. The team understood the importance of the prototyping process and adopted the “fail fast, learn fast” mindset as our primary objective in the prototyping process.

The “fail fast, learn fast” methodology allows the team to quickly fail, learn from our mistakes and continuously improve on the prototype. This methodology fits our use case as the team has the creative liberties on the design aspect of the application and most importantly, the freedom to fail. IBM mentioned that the costliest failure that an individual can make would be investing in a project that your customers don't want.

The team revisited the prototype that we had come out with yesterday and discovered some areas of improvement. The team surveyed our peers on each prototype, with the majority of responses highlighting what they liked about each prototype. In prototype 1, the screen to ask the user which causes they are passionate about is a great feature. While in prototype 2, the flexibility to cancel or edit the amount of monthly donation is the most desired feature amongst those surveyed, as per our requirement gathering process. Taking this feedback into account, the team will come out with prototype 3 to test our hypothesis that the user would experience a seamless flow from when clicking the button to enter the auto donate page, to donating to a charity, to looking at their donation history, to cancelling / editing the monthly donation amount. The following shows the adjustment that the team will be making for prototype 3.

As seen in the image, one of the screens in prototype 2 will be removed in favour of its prototype 1 counterpart. The goal tracking in prototype 1 will then be merged with the first screen in prototype 2, adding the goal tracking function. The remaining screens will be present in the application. The team unanimously voted to finish prototype 3 next Monday. The team also took note that there might be further changes to prototype 3 as inspiration comes to us over the weekend. The team will also be performing more research as set forth in the meeting minutes action items. The link to view the meeting minutes can be found here Meeting Minutes.pdf 1. Do note that you have to be signed in to your UofG email to access the document.

Our priority for next week would be to come out with a further iterated prototype, receive comments on the prototype from the clients and move towards interactive prototype to produce a hi-fi prototype. The team is proud of the team’s current progress and will continue this drive next week.

Day 5 | 24 July 2023


As planned, today the team worked on creating our third prototype, Prototype 3, by incorporating the user feedback from our peers that were based on our existing designs from Prototype 1 and 2. The best parts of each of the two older prototypes were merged in our latest iteration of the design, while keeping the goal of a intuitive interface and a seamless experience for our users in mind. The photo of Prototype 3 can be seen here:

It starts with the first two screens of Prototype 1 that allows users to access the Donation page via the existing Barcalys application interface as the intended design would be integrated with it. As favoured by our surveyees, the users would be prompted by a preferences screen for which causes they wish to donate to on their first access of the donation page. The user's selections would dictate what would be recommended by the algorithm to appear on their home screen. The home screen would be a revised version of screens from both Prototype 1 and 2. It would feature the accumulation of the user's donations as ideated in Prototype 2, along with the Purchases section where users are able to shop for items where the money would be donated to the related causes.

Based on the user's preferences, users would be able to view the related campaigns that would be recommended to the user featuring a Header, goad tracking, as well as an additional short description of the campaign as ideated in Prototype 1. The images pertaining to the causes would still be included which would hopefully invoke empathy for the parties in need. Upon selecting which donation fund they wish to donate to, they will be prompted with a options selection screen as featured in Prototype 1. This is where the users will be able to select the amount they wish to donate, whether it be a percentage of their income or a custom amount and also the monthly recurring donation option.

The Donation Subscriptions page of Prototype 2 will be included to provide users an overview of the donations that they are currently “subscribed” to. Here the ability to view the amounts that they are donating and edit them is retained as it was a desired feature of Prototype 2. There would also be the option to cancel their subscriptions anytime if they wish to so that there is no pressure on the user. This is essentially a combination of the My Donation Page & Account page in Prototype 2.

Finally, the History Page will allow users to view their Donation history while also being able to easily select an option to donate again if they wish to.

By combining the most desired features of our team's ideated prototypes based on the feedback provided by our surveyed users, we were able to come up with a more cohesive reiteration of our design. The next steps for us in the coming days would be to now gather more user feedback regarding our new Prototype 3 to reiterate another prototype if needed and progress towards an interactive hi-fi prototype of our refined design.
